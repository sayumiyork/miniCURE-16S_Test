    • 8.4 Try it Question 1 - What has more impact on human microbial variation, diet or individuality
      • 8.4.1 Step 1A: Plot high-level taxonomy (Phylum level) for each timepoint using the plot_bar() function.** Specifically, your plot should show Phyla diversity across all 5 timepoints.
      • 8.4.2 Step 1B: Plot high-level taxonomy (Phylum level) for each individual, using only normalized homogenized diet “HD” timepoints to minimize contribution of diet to the analysis using the plot_bar() function. Specifically, your plot should show Phyla diversity across all 21 individuals at HD timepoints.
    • 8.5 Try it Question 2 - What are some of the most abundant microbes when viewed at different taxonomic resolutions
      • 8.5.1 Step 2A. Plot Phyla across all subjects and identify the most abundant Phyla.** Specifically, your plot should show Phyla diversity across all 21 individuals.
      • 8.5.2 Step 2B. Subset the most abundant Phyla, and plot Order (Skip Class)
    • 8.6 Try it Question 3 - Does gender contribute to individual microbiome variation
      • 8.6.1 Step 3C. Test gender-based difference in Species for the low abundant phylum you selected.** Specifically, your plot should show species diversity within male and female groups for only your phylum of choice.
      • 8.6.2 Step 3D: Plot the individual abundance of 2 candidate microbes for gender differences based on your analysis above, and/or the curated menu of microbes below (previously associated with gender variation), with color denoting gender.
    • 8.7 Try it Question 4 - Does age contribute to individual microbiome variation
      • 8.7.1 Step 4A. Perform an initial survey to see if there are any differences in phyla related to age.
      • 8.7.2 Step 4B. Merge samples by age, then re-normalize and finally re-plot Phyla by age.
      • 8.7.3 Step 4C. Candidate Phyla implicated in age variation include Firmicutes and Bacteroidetes. Plot these specific phyla on their own, separately, to observe any shift that occurs with age.** Use your new merged and normalized ‘merge’ phyloseq object as input.

    • 9.4 Try it Question 1 - How sensitive is microbial diversity to variables like diet, age and gender
      • 9.4.1 Step 1A. Plot alpha diversity of the full MISO dataset by subject (individuals).
      • 9.4.2 Step 1B. Plot alpha diversity of the full MISO dataset, by timepointgroup.
      • 9.4.3 Step 1C. Plot alpha diversity of the full MISO dataset by age.
      • 9.4.4 Step 1D. Plot alpha diversity of the full MISO dataset by gender.
      • 9.4.5 Step 2. Plot alpha diversity of the full MISO dataset, grouping based on levels for 5 different metabolites: Creatinine, PCS, IS, HIPP, PAG.
      • 9.4.6 Step 3. Select data for poster and include the code used to generate the figure.
    • 9.5 Try it Question 2 - Do diet, age, gender and levels of metabolites correlate with microbe variation between individuals
      • 9.5.1 Step 1. Make a PcOA plot, ordinating on the entire miso dataset, and coloring by individual. Investigate resulting plot shape.
      • 9.5.2 Step 2. Using the PCoA plot from Step 1 above, look for any correlations between the shape of your PCoA plot and diet, gender and age
      • 9.5.3 Step 3. Using the PCoA plot from Step 1, look for any correlations between the shape of your PCoA plot and the level of metabolites.
      • 9.5.4 Step 4. One way of checking if the metabolite levels indeed correlate with your data, is to see if subsetting smaller chunks or specific chunks of the data will still maintain the metabolite-data relationship or break it.
    • 9.6 Try it Question 3 - What microbes (ASVs) differ between males and females, and does age have an impact
      • 9.6.1 Step 3A. Perform differential abundance analysis between genders.
      • 9.6.2 Step 3B. Determine the impact of younger age on differential abundance in males and females, by subsetting age < 50 data from the phyloseq object, and performing DESeq2 analysis on this subset.
      • 9.6.3 Step 3C. Determine the impact of older age on differential abundance in males and females, by subsetting age >= 50 data from the phyloseq object, and performing DESeq2 analysis on this subset.
      • 9.6.4 Step 4. Select data for poster and include the code used to generate the figure
    • 9.7 Try it Question 4 - Is there an interaction between diet and age and the microbiome
      • 9.7.1 Step 1. Plot alpha diversity based on age for all dietary groups, and then for BD, HD and WO.
      • 9.7.2 Step 2. Perform differential abundance analysis comparing HD to BD for the population in general, and then for older individuals specifically (because older individuals produced the biggest diet–age interaction based on alpha diversity).
      • 9.7.3 Step 2B. Perform differential abundance analysis between HD and BD for older individual (> 50 yo.)
      • 9.7.4 Step 3. Select data for poster and include the code used to generate the figure.
